"Homemade" Multitouch Table

We wanted to have a multitouch table to develop interesting interface ideas and games but the R&D budget didn’t allowed us to buy one so we decided to sacrifice a couple of weekends and around £200 to build one.

We chose the FTIR technique, soldered together almost a hundred infrared LEDs, placed them around a framed acrylic sheet and used a projector and a prepared PS-Eye with its IR filter removed to make it work. The image of the camera was processed by NUI Group’s CCV that broadcasted TUIO messages, containing all the touch information, on a socket to our Flash and Processing clients that used the data signal as user input.
